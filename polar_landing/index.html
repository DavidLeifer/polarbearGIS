Polarbear GIS


A pipeline for Raster climate data


El Nino and La Nina is said to control precipitation and temperature in the United States Southeast and Pacific North West in what is known as the El Nino- Southern Oscillation (ENSO). El Nino 3.4 is an index based on a running mean of sea surface temperatures along the equator in the mid Pacific Ocean.

Temperature correlated with El Nino 3.4 Index
The first web app displays the correlation between El Nino 3.4 index and a raster of gridded measurements of temperature.

ANOVA between TEMP El Nino, La Nina, Neutral grouped means

Precipitation correlated with El Nino 3.4 Index
The second web app does the same but for precipitation. Quantile breaks are calculated to better visualize the data.

ANOVA between PPT El Nino, La Nina, Neutral grouped means

Mean Temperature and Precipitation for Winter Classes
The third displays mean temperature and precipitation grouped by El Nino, La Nina, and Neutral January means for 33 years of data (1981-2014).
Difference Between El Nino/Neutral and La Nina/Neutral
The fourth app displays the difference between La Nino and Neutral mean January temperatures and precipitation. It also shows the difference between El Nino and Neutral January mean.
2019 Polar Vortex: Twitter Sentiment and Weather Radar
The fifth app displays timeseries radar data and sentiment analysis of geolocated tweets about the storm on January 29th.
