Polarbear GIS


A GIS data pipeline for Raster climate data


El Nino and La Nina is said to control precipitation and temperature in the United States Southeast and Pacific North West in what is known as the El Nino- Southern Oscillation (ENSO). El Nino 3.4 is an index based on a running mean of sea surface temperatures along the equator in the mid Pacific Ocean. Our first web app displays the correlation between El Nino 3.4 index and a raster of gridded measurements of temperature. The second web app does the same but for precipitation. The third displays mean temperature and precipitation grouped by El Nino, La Nina, and Neutral January means for 33 years of data (1981-2014). The fourth app displays timeseries radar data and sentiment analysis of geolocated tweets about the storm on January 29th.
